About Me

I believe physical AI will reshape the world. I'm building toward that.

The gap between simulation and the real world is where I work.

Most AI lives in servers. I'm interested in the kind that has to deal with a wet floor, a drifting sensor, and an obstacle that wasn't on the map. Embodied AI, robots that perceive, decide, and act in the physical world, is the hardest version of the intelligence problem, and the one that matters most.

My work spans the full Embodied AI training pipeline: building environments in Isaac Sim, training policies in Isaac Lab, and pushing toward real-world deployment through sim-to-real transfer. Mobile robots, manipulators, humanoids, quadrupeds, aerial systems. I'm interested in all of it.
